Credits

The starting point for this app was a joint project performed with Scott Otto, Dave Kloepper, and Justin Douglas for the University of Minnesota Data Visualizaton and Analytics Boot Camp. The work presented here are my contributions to this project. I used the original Excel spreadsheet from the other project team members to rebuild the database. Any errors in the data or resulting app are my own.

The Voronoi diagram was created using the JavaScript library Turf. This library is a useful collection of functions for spatial analysis.

Data on snowfall was pulled from NOAA - Daily Snow and Ice.

The Minneapolis Snow Emergency data was pulled from the City of Minneapolis via the opendataminneapolis API. The API supports queries and downloads in several formats including csv and geoJSON.

The storm information leading to snow emergencies was manually scraped from NOAA's National Center for Environmental Information Storm Events Database

Similar storm summaries can be found from the Weather Prediction Center Event Review.

Finally, another source of winter storm information that could be scraped is the MN Department of Natural Resources' Climate Journal.

The surface analysis and satellite images were scraped with Beautiful Soup from the National Weather Service's Weather Prediction Center Archive.

Future Work

Most of the quantities used in the tables and plots are computed each time the graphic or table is created. All of these could be computed ahead of time and stored in a database to be queried to improve the responsiveness of the app. For example, the snowfall Voronoi diagrams and the towing and ticketing choropleths slow the response of the app when the emergency is changed or towing vs ticketing is switched.

There is much room for improvement in the layout of the app pages as well as the organization of the code. Some of the tables and plots are created using a Flask app route specific to that table when a generic function could have been created to cover multiple cases.
